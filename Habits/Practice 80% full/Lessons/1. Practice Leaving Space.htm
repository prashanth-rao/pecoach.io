What does 80% full really mean?

80% full isn't a real number. It's not a thing you can truly quantify.

80% full is an idea.

It means:

Eating until you're just satisfied. And, at times, if you're trying to lose weight/fat, eating a little less than you want. Maybe even walking away feeling a teeny tiny bit un-satisfied, sometimes.

Leaving a little space at the end of a meal.

Finishing a meal feeling light and energized, instead of heavy and sluggish.

Changing your expectations of what a meal looks like, and feels like when you're done.

Your "80% full" goals

There are two goals:

Learn what "80% full"...

·         looks like for you. Approximately how much food will get you to feeling 80% full? How many hand portion sizes?

·         feels like for you. Can you put some names to the feelings, to be more dialed-in to knowing when you hit the mark? What changes do you feel in your body, emotions, and thoughts as you hit 80% full?

Eating to 80% full is about experimenting with changing your expectations of eating and fullness.

Over time, ideally you'll shift from planning for 80% full (e.g., with deliberately adjusting portion sizes) towards sensing 80% full.

To do that, you need to experiment, repeat, and observe, over and over.

Stopping takes practice. Try to purposely plan to stop eating early. Then gather data on what happens.

Things to keep in mind

Learning to eat to 80% full isn't about depriving yourself, or making "rules". It means learning about yourself and your own unique cues.

1.   Take just a little bit less than you normally would.

Take whatever portion you normally eat and have 2/3 to 3/4 of that instead.

2.   Eat slowly.

Put your fork down between bites. You can also time yourself, if you like. Set a baseline and try to add 1-2 minutes per meal.

3.   Notice and name what happens when you do this.

When you purposely eat a smaller portion, you're experimenting with changing the experience of eating.

Observe. Gather data.

Over time, you'll start to intuitively sense where 80% full is. But for now, start with deliberately adjusting portion sizing.

4.   Help yourself remember.

Consider sticking a note where you'll see it while eating, or preparing food (so you remember to take 2/3 to 3/4 of the normal portion).
