Smart self-experiments: A no-fat day.

Focus on learning

The purpose of self-experiment days like this is for exploration and discovery.

They're a study of the interactions between food and the way you think, feel, and act.

They're a study of:

  • your body
  • your habits
  • your needs
  • your likes and dislikes

At the end of the experiment, ask yourself:

1. What was this experience like for me?

2. What did this experience teach me about myself and what works for me?

This experiment isn't meant to convince you that a certain way of eating is "bad" or "good". The focus is testing, observing, and learning, temporarily.

Know yourself

For one day: Eat zero added fats and foods high in fat.

The goal for this experiment is to keep fat intake as low as it can possibly go, and to see what happens.

What to eat

For this experiment, you may choose:

Any vegetable (except high-fat veggies like avocados and olives)

  • Any fruit
  • Any whole grain
  • Any very lean protein

For very lean protein, you might include:

  • skinless chicken breast
  • 98% extra lean beef (with fat drained off)
  • pork tenderloin
  • very lean fish, such as tuna, cod, tilapia, haddock, etc.
  • seafood, such as shrimp, crab, mussels, scallops, lobster, octopus or calamari (not fried)
  • egg whites
  • protein powder (except hemp protein powder, which often contains fat)
  • beans and legumes
  • fat-free dairy products

What to exclude during the experiment

Avoid:

  • fattier cuts of meat or fish (e.g., salmon)
  • whole eggs or egg yolks
  • higher-fat dairy (e.g., higher-fat yogurt, cream, cheese, butter)
  • nuts and seeds
  • avocado
  • coconut
  • oils, cooking sprays
  • sauces and dressings containing oil

How to cook low fat

Cooking methods that are low-fat friendly are steaming, broiling, boiling, braising, poaching, or baking. You can also sauté in water or stock.

Alternatively, try to avoid high-fat cooking methods like frying, sautéing, grilling or roasting with oil.

Record your data

Throughout the day, make notes about your experiment.

  • What did you notice in general about eating this way?
  • How did you feel and perform?
  • What was it like to shop, prepare, and cook food for this experiment day?
  • What did you learn about yourself and your body eating this way?

Record whatever you notice. Add that information to your Owner's Manual.

This is an experiment. Think like a scientist.
