Smart self-experiments: A low-carb day

Focus on learning

The purpose of self-experiment days like this is for exploration and discovery.

They're a study of the interactions between food and the way you think, feel, and act.

They're a study of:

  • your body
  • your habits
  • your needs
  • your likes and dislikes

At the end of the experiment, ask yourself:

1. What was this experience like for me?

2. What did this experience teach me about myself and what works for me?

This experiment isn't meant to convince you that a certain way of eating is "bad" or "good”. The focus is testing, observing, and learning, temporarily.

Know yourself

For one day: Eat zero added carbs and foods high in carbs.

The goal for this experiment is to keep your carbohydrate intake as low as it can possibly go, and to see what happens.

What to eat

For this experiment, you may choose:

Any non-starchy vegetable, such as:

  • leafy greens
  • green string beans
  • tomatoes
  • peppers
  • onions/garlic
  • cucumber
  • mushrooms
  • eggplant

Any type of protein (except higher-carb proteins like dairy or beans/legumes):

  • poultry
  • fish
  • beef
  • pork
  • eggs

Any type of fat or oil, such as:

  • olive oil
  • butter
  • unsweetened nut butter

Any type of low-carb, high-fat foods, such as:

  • nuts
  • seeds
  • avocados
  • coconut/coconut milk
  • heavy whipping cream (36% fat or higher)
  • high-fat cheeses (such as goat cheese, Brie or Camembert)

What to avoid during the experiment

Try to avoid all sugars and starches, including:

  • grains (e.g., corn, rice, oats, wheat, quinoa)
  • grain-based products (e.g., pasta, noodles, cereal, flour)
  • baked goods (e.g., breads, muffins, pastries)
  • beans and legumes
  • fruits
  • starchy vegetables (e.g., potatoes, sweet potatoes, yuca, taro / tapioca)
  • sweetened drinks (e.g., juice, soda, sweetened non-dairy milks like soy milk)
  • most dairy (e.g., whey protein, milk, yogurt, cottage cheese, cream cheese) except butter or heavy cream (36% fat or higher)
  • sugar (e.g., in your coffee or tea)

Record your data

Throughout the day, make notes about your experiment.

  • What did you notice in general about eating this way?
  • How did you feel and perform?
  • What was it like to shop, prepare, and cook food for this experiment day?
  • What did you learn about yourself and your body eating this way?

Record whatever you notice. Add that information to your Owner's Manual.

This is an experiment. Think like a scientist.
