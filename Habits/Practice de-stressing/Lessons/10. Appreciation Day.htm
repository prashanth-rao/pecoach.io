Appreciation Day

Spread the love and say thanks.

Today, spread the love and say thanks.

Making progress in health is never a trip you take alone. Even if it may feel that way sometimes, no one is an island. Other people and community have made vital contributions.

So let's appreciate them and say thanks.

Gratitude

Look around your life, right now. Who's helped you get to where you are?

  • Maybe your partner helped with food prep.
  • Maybe your friend worked out with you.
  • Maybe your dog was thrilled to go for walks with you.
  • Maybe your kid took your progress photos.
  • Maybe someone encouraged you when you were feeling bummed out.

Make a list of all the people who have helped you along your journey, even in tiny ways—maybe without realizing it.

Take a moment to feel grateful for these people and their help.

Say thank you

Practice saying thanks to the people who have helped you. Tell them exactly how they helped you.

"Hey Alex, I really appreciate you hanging out with me at the gym. You always push me to get that last rep. Thanks, friend."

"Bud, I want you to know how much I appreciate you helping me out with the grocery shopping. I know it's a lot of work, and it's been a big help to me. Thanks."

"Billy, mom and dad are really glad you know how to use the digital camera, 'cause I can't figure the freaking thing out! If it wasn't for you, I wouldn't be able to record all this progress! Thanks kiddo!"

Appreciate through action

Saying "thanks" is a great start.

If you can, go one step further. Show your appreciation through action. Do something to show gratitude, even if it's small.

  • Help someone else out.
  • Take a gym newbie under your wing.
  • Treat your loved one(s) to something special.
  • Bring someone a thank you gift.

Find one person to whom you can show appreciation through action. Walk the talk. Pay it forward.

And enjoy the health benefits of gratitude.
