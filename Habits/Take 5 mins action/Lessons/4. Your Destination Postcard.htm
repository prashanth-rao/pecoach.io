Your Destination Postcard

Where do you want to go in this coaching program?

What do you want?

A change process often starts with thinking about what we don't want. For instance:

  • I don't want to be carrying excess body fat.
  • I don't want to feel out of shape.
  • I don't want to take all these medications.
  • I don't want to feel like a foreigner in the gym.
  • I don't want all these injuries.
  • I don't want to feel unattractive and unsexy.
  • I don't want to feel out of control of my eating.

    • That's a good start. AND, it's not totally clear yet what the destination is. Defining what you want goes deeper than thinking about avoiding what you don't want.

    For example:

  • I want to be lean and healthy, with some dense bones and strong muscle.
  • I want to be in better shape — strong, fast, flexible, agile, physically capable.
  • I want to take fewer medications, or get off my medications entirely.
  • I want to feel comfortable and confident moving and being active.
  • I want to feel healthy, mobile, functional, and pain-free. I want to thrive, feeling deeply healthy.
  • I want to feel like a stone cold fox.
  • I want to eat normally, intuitively, and sanely. I want to nourish my body with wise food choices.

    • That feels way more inspiring.

    Those visions also start to give a much clearer blueprint for taking action.

    Today, come up with your Destination Postcard.

    Imagine you could get a postcard from the future that shows exactly what you want to happen with your coaching journey.

    The "Future You" arrived at the destination and sent a postcard back in time telling you all about it.

    In a year from today, in an ideal world...

    1. Where do you want to be?

    2. What do you want to be doing?

    3. What do you want to feel?

    4. What adventures do you want to be having?

    Whether it's climbing a mountain, biking across the world, going cliff diving, winning a competition, feeling like a ninja, or just fitting in to your old pants... imagine who and where you could be one year from now.

    Then capture that.

    Clip photos. Make a Pinterest board. Put up a Carpe Diem calendar. Draw a picture. Call up a friend and make a ridiculous bet. Surf the web to find inspiration.

    Find a little object that you'll use on your adventures (e.g., snorkel, tropical vacation bottle opener, Conan-the-Barbarian-style broadsword, hockey puck, your kid's picture, a sweatband, whatever) and set it by your computer as a reminder.

    Write it down. Or, write a letter to your future self and get it delivered to yourself a year from now.

    However you choose to envision your future:

    Imagine it.

    Capture it.

    Have fun with it.

    The coaching journey is about using every day to practice getting one day closer to making the destination real.
