An Information Vacation

Practice tuning-out of media to tune-in to you.

Less information = better body.

We live in an information-rich society. It's never been so quick and easy to get and consume information.

Thanks to the fitness media, we know about every diet and workout plan on earth.

But are we happier? More relaxed? More focused? More confident about our decisions?

Are our bodies and minds better for it?

Arguably, no. On all counts.

It's not simply more information that makes us better, or more options.

These things do create more brain chatter and less focus.

It's more deliberate, conscious choices and actions, consistently, that makes us better.

Take an information vacation

Practice tuning-out of media, in any ways that you can. Experiment with taking in less.

What do you discover on your information vacation?

  • What's challenging?
  • What's easy?
  • What changes?
  • What do you learn about yourself?

What's good?

Where possible, focus on the benefits of this information vacation.

Such as potentially:

  • having more time (imagine what you could do with those extra minutes or hours)
  • taking more action
  • feeling more calm and focused
  • doing less "compare and despair" with others' lifestyles or bodies

What happens when you try to shift focus from "knowing" and "thinking" to "feeling" and "doing"?
