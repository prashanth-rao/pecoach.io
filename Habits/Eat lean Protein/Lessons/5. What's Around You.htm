What's Around You?

Change your environment, change your body.

What to do today

·         Notice how the spaces you live in, and the people around you, affect your decisions.

For instance:

    • What's around you in your physical environment, such as your home, workplace, or community?
    • Who's around you in your social environment, such as friends, family, roommates, or coworkers?

·         Consider how you might make small adjustments in your environment to improve your decisions so that you don't need as much "willpower".

You don't have to change anything immediately. Just start by noticing.

What's around us shapes our choices.

We make literally hundreds of food decisions every day.

Coffee or tea? Peanut butter or jam? Should I start or finish this taco?

We're not aware that we're making most of those decisions. We often can't explain them. We're not consciously choosing.

We just… make them. We sort of "find ourselves" somewhere, or doing something.

We just float along, like leaves down a river, with the people around us. The situation. Our habits. Familiar routines.

This isn’t wrong. It's pretty normal.

But, it means that we sometimes don't realize when our environment (such as the things and people around us) shape our choices.

In fact, our environment drives our eating and activity behaviors way more than we realize.

Make your environment work for you.

The good news? By changing your environment—even just a little bit—you can put yourself back in control.

Notice and name your cues.

Don't worry about changing your whole routine and environment right now.

Today, just notice and name what elements of your environment are helping you or hindering you.

  • Your schedule?
  • Your route to work or school?
  • The people around you?
  • Food advertisements?
  • The lunch room or communal candy jar at work?
  • Your morning, lunchtime, or evening rituals?
  • What's in your cupboards or fridge?
  • What's near you? (e.g., gyms, grocery stores, walking trails, etc.)
  • Social media?
  • Etc.

Look around. Observe. Pay attention.

Stay aware of what you're seeing, doing, and experiencing.
