Chill Out

How relaxation can improve our overall outlook on life, health, and performance.

Coach's Notes: The role of relaxation in health.

Too much stress and anxiety can be like a parking brake on your performance.

Think of two scenarios when you had to perform a task.

1.   First, remember a time where you were very anxious, maybe rushing, or a time when you were so stressed out and tense about doing a good job you were practically vibrating.

2.   Now, remember a time where you felt relaxed and open, totally focused and "in the zone", having a great time.

In which scenario did you perform better?

In which scenario did you feel better?

In which scenario was the outcome more successful?

While it could seem like focusing on the stressful elements of a situation would help you do better, the opposite is true.

While a little bit of stress sharpens our attention, too much is like trying to drive forward with the parking break on.

There's a zone of "optimal stress"—neither too much nor too little.

How to actively chill out

Here's an easy way to start relaxing:

Focus on your breathing.

Try this exercise before getting to sleep:

  • Lie down comfortably on your back. Close your eyes.
  • Put one hand on your belly and one hand on your chest. (You can also use a pillow on your stomach, if you like.)
  • First, focus on what the belly hand is doing. Ideally, the belly hand is rising up and down gently as you breathe.
  • Then, focus on what the other hand is doing. Ideally, the chest hand is still. A sign that you're breathing deeply from a fully engaged abdomen rather than your chest.
  • Breathe in—belly expands, hand goes up. Try to expand your belly to the ceiling.
  • Breathe out—belly lowers, hand goes down. Try to push your navel to the ground.
  • Take 20 slow, mindful "belly breaths". Don't rush.

Breathing is always with you. Use this technique anytime.

Use this technique anytime you want to relax, standing or sitting. It can take as little as 30 seconds or less to chill and focus on your breath.

Use it with eating. Take a few deep breaths before each meal. And if you find yourself rushing during the meal, stop for a couple of belly breaths.
