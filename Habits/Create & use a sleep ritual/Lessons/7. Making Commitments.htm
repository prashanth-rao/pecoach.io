Making Commitments

Maintaining meaningful commitments.

Commitment is saying: I WILL do this.

When you commit to something, you make participation non-negotiable.

For example, you might...

Commit to doing what matters.

You do the daily practices and essential behaviors that you know are getting you where you want to go.

Commit to confronting your challenges.

You keep going even when you face setbacks. You persist in the face of fears and obstacles. You face the difﬁcult-difﬁcult. You're in the commitment for the long haul.

Commit to yourself.

You're making a commitment to prioritize what's best for your health. In turn, being a better version of yourself serves your family, friends, and people closest to you.

Do you want to refresh your commitment?

Every day (every moment) is a chance to renew your commitment to yourself, your goals, or your coaching journey.

What got you in this to begin with? What are the deepest reasons you're doing what you're doing? Are you still driven by that?

What's changed? What can you shift? What's non-negotiable? What's so important to you that you're staying relentlessly committed to it?
