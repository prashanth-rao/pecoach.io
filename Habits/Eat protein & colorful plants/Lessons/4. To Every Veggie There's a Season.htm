To Every Veggie There's a Season

Buy in season for the healthiest and freshest produce.

Coach's Notes: Buying and eating fruits and veggies in season.

Why buy vegetables and fruits in season?

  • They're fresher.
  • They taste better.
  • They're more nutritious.
  • They're more sustainable.
  • For all of that, they’re sometimes also cheaper.

When you buy seasonal produce from local, small-farm providers:

  • You support your local community and economy.
  • You directly support our societies' keystone workers (and often lowest-paid workers)—farmers.
  • You get to know who grows your food, and maybe something about the work that goes into growing and sourcing that food.
  • You cut down on fossil fuel use from food transport and refrigeration.
  • You save money by cutting out waste and cost mark-ups from food processing.

For the next few days, try to make as many of your meals as seasonally appropriate as possible. You could use this opportunity to try a new vegetable, fruit, or recipe for something in season. Enjoy the local variety.
