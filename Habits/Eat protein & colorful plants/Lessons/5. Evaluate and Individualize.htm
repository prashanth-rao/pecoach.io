Start tailoring your nutrition plan to you

When it comes to nutrition, here are two basic principles.

1.   Everyone is the same.

2.   Everyone is different.

Well, that clears up the confusion...

Everyone is the same

Human physiology works pretty much the same for everyone.

Coaching fundamentals

·         Do one thing at a time.

·         Eat a variety of real, whole, unprocessed foods that add value to your body.

·         To lose fat, eat a little bit less.

·         Make sure your overall eating environment, practices and mindset help you rather than hinder you.

·         Aim for consistent and "pretty good" every day, rather than extreme swings from rigid or "perfect" and uncontrolled or chaotic.

·         Make minimum commitments to new practices. Do them consistently for at least 2 weeks before making any changes, to determine how it's working for you.

·         Your body reflects what you put into it (food, recovery) and take out of it (activity, stress).

·         Be an expert of your experience. Make decisions based on data, close observation of yourself, and your goals. Rather than rules or someone else's plan for you.

Mastering these fundamentals and practicing them consistently is what leads to mastery.

Everyone is different

While it's mostly right to say that people are fundamentally the same, there are for sure important preferences and variables that make us unique. Those unique characteristics show up in both people, foods, and our relationship between them.

For example, maybe...

  • Some foods make you feel better than others.
  • Some foods don't make you feel very good at all (especially if you have a food sensitivity, intolerance, or allergy).
  • You feel better with more or less fat, more or less meat, cooked vegetables instead of fresh, different types of carbohydrates, and so forth.
  • Some foods (or food environments, or situations) "trigger" you into behaviors that aren't healthy.

Keep it simple

Individualizing and refining doesn’t mean you get bogged down in tiny, probably irrelevant, details. It doesn’t mean spreadsheets and elaborate, restrictive plans.

In fact, individualizing often means making things simpler.

Getting down to the essence of what YOU need. What YOU can do in your real life.

Simply make small, practical, and sustainable nutritional adjustments based on careful, thoughtful, informed self-awareness.

Steer dynamically

Think about how you drive a car.

How do you know what "works"?

Ask yourself:

  • Do you feel good inside and out?
  • Do you have lots of energy for what you want to do?
  • Are your health indicators (e.g., blood chemistry, blood pressure, resting heart rate, immune system) good?
  • Are you performing well in the gym?
  • Are you meeting your goals?

Ignore what you think you "should" do. Ignore other people's "rules of the road".

Stay in your lane, driving your car to your destination.

Stay relaxed and moving in the right general direction.
