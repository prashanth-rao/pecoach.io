Self-experiment: Eat like our ancestors

An ancestral diet (also known as a Paleo or Primal-style eating) involves eating only whole foods that early humans could have hunted, dug up, and/or gathered.

What to eat on an ancestral diet

Any animal, including:

  • any and all land and sea animals: (chicken, turkey, duck, beef, pork, fish, seafood, wild game, etc.)
  • any eggs (chicken, duck, fish, etc.)

Almost all plants, including:

  • any vegetable (whether those are leaves, stems, starchy roots, etc.)
  • any fruit
  • any seeds or plant sugar that is edible when raw (such as nuts, coconuts, honey, maple syrup)

Limit your sweets like honey and fresh/dried fruit. Prehistoric fruit was rarely very sweet, and in most places, only available in season.

Eat less of:

  • grains or grain products (such as bread or pasta)
  • processed dairy such as cheese, ice cream, or yogurt
  • beans or legumes
  • sugar or artificial sweeteners
  • any other processed foods such as soy milk, protein powder, diet sodas

Make this experiment work for you

Ancestral diets are diverse. The fundamental thing they have in common is that they're made of foods that early humans could have acquired.

What that looks like for you specifically, is up to you.

Plant-based eater?

That can work. Most prehistoric people wouldn't manage to eat animals every single day either.

However, there's a challenge: You have to figure out how to get some protein without grains, beans, legumes, processed dairy, or protein powders.

It won't be perfect. That's fine.

This is about experimentation, not perfection.

Do I have to eat it all raw?

Nah. Early humans had fire—and tough stomachs. It's OK to cook. (Unless you want to go out for sashimi or beef tartare. Your call.)

What counts as a whole food?

You have some leeway here.

  • You can include nut butter or cold-pressed extra-virgin oils.
  • You can eat the meat you like… no need to feel obligated to eat seal liver or whale blubber.
  • You can eat minimally processed whole foods, such as frozen vegetables.

What should I make?

You may already be well accustomed to eating ancestral-style. Some common ancestral meals could look like...

Meal 1 – Eggs scrambled with vegetables and a bit of coconut oil

Meal 2 – Chicken coconut curry with vegetables

Meal 3 – Turkey meatballs + salsa with avocado

Meal 4 – Steak, salad with olive oil vinaigrette, and berries for dessert

Dang! That sounds really good!
