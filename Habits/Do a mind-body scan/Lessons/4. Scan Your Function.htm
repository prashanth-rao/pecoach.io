What do you notice?

As you do mind-body scans over the next few days, what signals is your body giving you about your coping style under stress?

Under stress, do you tend to over-function, or under-function?

Pause to self-reflect, and get insight into some deeper motivations.

  • What's good about your stress-coping style? How does it benefit and serve you? Are you seeing the results of your coping style show up in your body? Where are you seeing positive results?
  • What's bad about your stress-coping style? What does it cost you? How does it cost you in your body and deep health?
  • How does your stress-coping style affect your nutrition and exercise practices? How is stress affecting the choices you make on nutrition and exercise?

    • Pausing to direct towards just a little bit better

    When you're able to pause in the moment, gain perspective, and reflect on the bigger picture of a stressful situation, you can think more clearly.

    You can use that space to ask grounding questions like:

  • Is my body telling me that I'm dealing with this situation in the best possible way?
  • Right now, in this situation, how could I respond just a little bit better... so that I stay true to what's important to me, and do what honestly makes my body feel good?
  • Knowing what I know thus far about my body and brain, how can I help myself be slightly more awesome... right now?
