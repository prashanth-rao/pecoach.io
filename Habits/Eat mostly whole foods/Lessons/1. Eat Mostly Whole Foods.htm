Eat Mostly Whole Foods

You decide the rules of the game that will move you along the whole foods continuum.

Coach's Notes: Framing the whole food project as a game or experiment; suggestions for how to decide what whole foods are and how to move towards this goal.

You decide what will move you up the whole foods continuum.

Whole foods include:

  • vegetables
  • fruits
  • meats and poultry
  • fish and seafood
  • nuts and seeds
  • beans and lentils
  • whole intact grains
  • minimally processed dairy (e.g., fresh plain yogurt)

Experiment to move up the continuum

Try experimenting with some new whole foods. Have fun and use it as a way to step outside your comfort zone.

You could try to...

1.   Build your awareness of the difference between whole foods and processed foods.

In your opinion, what "counts" as whole foods? Why?

See if you can notice your own thinking, feeling, and reasoning. Notice where and when you feel resistant to "giving up" processed foods.

2.   Transition towards a "mostly whole foods" menu.

Try making some small changes, like taking a break from a few processed staples for awhile. Or, embrace the challenge of finding new whole-food recipes.

3.   Help yourself remember and execute.

Give yourself a reminder and check it often.

Stock up on delicious whole foods in the next few days so that choosing whole foods is simple and convenient.

4.   Build a whole foods environment.

What’s in your kitchen? What whole foods do you need to add? What processed foods need to go?

Stock your kitchen, work space, or any other place that you can with convenient whole foods.

You decide what “mostly” is, according to your body, your life, and your goals.

Play with that baseline to see what you can improve a little bit.

  • If you’re eating 50% whole foods now, try for 60%.
  • If you’re eating 80% whole foods and love a challenge, shoot for 100% for two weeks.
  • If you’re an astronaut, eat one of the little sprouts you’re growing in zero-G.
