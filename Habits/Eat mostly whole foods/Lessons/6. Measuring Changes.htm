Measuring Changes

Time to reflect on your progress.

What's changed for you recently?

Thinking back over the last few months, year, or couple of years, how are you a different person now than you used to be?

Do you move differently now than you used to?

Do you eat differently now?

Do you tackle your challenges differently now?

Do you feel different—maybe in a hard-to-define kinda way?

Do you not feel different at all? (Which is interesting too.)

What's progress?

How do you define and measure "change"?

If you want to improve your skill at making deliberate change, what do you need to do?

To know how to measure progress, you'll have to start with a solid understanding of the fundamentals as a baseline.

Fundamentals like:

  • Who are you?
  • What's important to you?
  • What do you stand for?
  • Why are you doing this?

Then, you can be more specific in answering the question:

  • How will you know youre making progress?

Some examples, beyond the numbers

Of course, you can track your progress by looking at:

  • your consistency with daily practices, nutrition and movement
  • your measurements
  • your photos

You can look at a lot of other things too. For instance, reflecting back on change you've made recently:

  • Are you doing the right things, consistently, every day?
  • Are you focused on meaningful actions—doing what truly matters? (Or are you feeling stuck in "wondering and worrying"?)
  • Have you grown as a person? Have you "stretched" and challenged yourself mentally and emotionally?
  • Have you developed new life skills?
  • Have you taken control of your life and responsibility for your actions?
  • Do you think proactively? Do you now anticipate, plan, and strategize?
  • How is your daily-life movement quality? Are you pain free and mobile?
  • Are you more physically confident? Willing to try new things—even if it means struggling or feeling silly? Do you walk tall?

These things are intangible. Tough to measure. But they're there.

And they're just as important as—sometimes even more important than—getting leaner and fitter.

Think about how you're different now.

What's changed? What's better?

Why are those changes important to you?

Think about it.
