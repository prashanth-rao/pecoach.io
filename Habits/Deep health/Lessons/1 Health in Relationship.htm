Health in Relationship

Health depends on mutually caring and supportive relationships.

Health in relationship.

To stay lean and healthy, you need to give back.

Science supports it. Scientists studying the genetics and molecular biology of cancer and other chronic diseases recently made a couple of interesting discoveries:

  • Loneliness and feeling alienated from other people tells our cells to produce inflammation and suppress our immune system.
  • Social connections do the opposite. Strong relationships and helping other people physically strengthen our bodies.

In other words, when someone feels bad and/or isolated, their cells and body chemistry show it. When they connect with and help others, their cells and body chemistry show that too.

Interestingly, it's not only thinking about relationships that keep cells healthy. It's acts of helping others and feeling connected in a meaningful way that creates the positive changes in body chemistry.

No one gets healthy in isolation. Lasting fitness and deep health are a shared project.

Think about a time in your past that you felt deeply that you wanted to make a lot of change. Were you nervous, frustrated or confused? Grumpy, intimidated or lost? Struggling to move your body, or sick and tired of being sick and tired?

How did that change happen alongside of other people? Who else was involved? A coach, friends and family, gym staff, or a thoughtful barista?

You can give back

Everyone is on a journey of figuring things out for themselves, in different ways. Everyone is working on mastering their own basic principles. We have a lot to learn from each other, and a lot to share.

You're an expert of your experience. You have unique experience, insight, and empathy to share.

You've done a lot, tried a lot, and learned a lot. You know how it feels to stumble through new practices and a new routine. It's very possible that some of that experience, insight, and empathy could help someone else.

It's all connected

Meaningful human relationships are crucial for health.

Everything psychological is ultimately biological. When we're feeling good, deeply, it's a sign that the whole body is functioning well. When bodies are functioning, they're more likely to stay lean, strong, and healthy for a whole amazing lifetime.

Even more, when people feel valued by others, they're more likely to value themselves. When people value themselves—their lives and their bodies—they're more likely to make positive, healthy choices.

When we support deep health in all dimensions, it's easier to eat well. A healthy fit body depends on healthy, fit relationships with the world around us.

You get what you give. When we do positive things in the world, it benefits our bodies, our lives… and everyone around us.
