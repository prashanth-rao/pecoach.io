How to come down from the high.

You know that moment after an awesome party, when everyone has left, and you're exhausted in a good way, and you start picking at the cake to calm down a bit.

After a big event, like any perceived milestone, it's expected to want a break. Breaks are healthy.

Give yourself a few days to celebrate, wind down, and maybe grieve this milestone a little bit.

It's a time for celebration and joy.

In those times, it can also be easy to let the wheels come off the bus, a little too much.

Don't let achievements be an excuse to lose healthy practices, routines, and focus.

Stay present, plan ahead

  • Stay aware. Stay present.
  • Notice and name your thoughts.
  • Understand that what you are feeling is normal.
  • Don't BS yourself with self-sabotaging thoughts and behaviors. Call them out.
  • Recognize what you need and want to move through this period. You may need to hibernate and be alone, party and reconnect, however you deal with these types of experiences.
  • Get out a calendar right now and mark a day two or three days after you finish coaching. That is the day you will return to a clear-eyed focus on good nutrition and activity practices.
  • Take a few days off if you like, then come back ready to roll.
