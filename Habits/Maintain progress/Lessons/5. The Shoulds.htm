The Shoulds

Don’t “should” all over yourself.

The Shoulds is a horrible disease, and nobody is immune.

People who have The Shoulds suffer mental and emotional torment. They may think that they...

  • should run (or [insert activity here])... even if they hate it... because that's what "fit people" do.
  • should look like a fitness magazine cover... even if that image is total BS... because that's what "fit people" look like.
  • should be leaner... even if it's not right for them, or even if they have made amazing progress… because that's what "fit people" are.
  • should have worked harder… even if they did their best… because that's what some imaginary athlete is probably doing right now.

The Shoulds is a nasty affliction.

You "compare and despair". You are convinced that everyone is better, smarter, fitter, leaner, more together, etc. than you.

You make "if-then" rules about the universe such as:

  • "If I lose 10 (20, 50, etc.) pounds, then I should be happy forever."
  • "If I have a perfect diet/training program, then I should have a perfect life."
  • "If I follow coaching instructions perfectly, then all my problems should go away." (Well, that part is true.)

There is a cure: What is.

What is rather than what should be.

What is, is real. It's who you truly are, in all your warty, majestic, flawed and gloriously human splendor.

It's this magnificent moment, right now.

It's the deliciousness of imperfection that is honestly experienced.

It's a celebration of gratitude and awareness.

To cure The Shoulds:

  • Take two "what IS" pills.
  • Drink plenty of fluids.
  • Check in to your real life.
  • Repeat daily.
