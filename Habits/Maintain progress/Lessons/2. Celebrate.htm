Celebrate

What have you accomplished recently?

Relentless optimism is the secret to long-term success.

There's a famous, highly-sought-after trainer and coach. He gets the best results of any trainer at his gym, and when asked his secret to success he says, "It's simple."

"I relentlessly point out and recognize client progress."

  • Client remembered a pen for their workout sheet? He'd call it out.
  • Client had a healthy breakfast before training? High fives.
  • Client did one more rep than last week? Yeah!

This coach would notice and name every tiny shred of progress and success, no matter how small. That might sound like useless fluff or unnecessary sucking up to the client.

There were two things that make this magical:

1.   The coach found real things to praise. The client did one more rep. That's a fact.

2.   The coach consistently focused on the good stuff and ignored the bad stuff. Over time, the client started behaving like a confident achiever… because the evidence told them that they were. The "filter" that the coach provided changed how the client felt, acted, and performed.

Because of this coach's approach, the clients kicked ass.

  • They thought more constructive thoughts. Which meant they were more mentally focused, committed, and prepared.
  • They felt more positive feelings. Which meant they were consistently motivated and energized.
  • They performed better, and made faster progress. Which meant they ran faster, hit harder, jumped higher, and lifted (or lost) more weight than they thought was possible.
  • They appreciated their unique strengths more. Which meant they took better care of themselves. They even learned to love their bodies.

Celebrate: It's science

Research in both coaching and neuroscience shows us that success recognition, celebration, and positive feedback works much better than self-criticism or constantly focusing on "failures" and "mistakes", for every goal.

This isn't just because positive feedback is inspiring (although it is). It's because success is the start of an action plan.

  • What went well? How can you do more of that?
  • What's already working? How can you make it work better?
  • What tells you that you're on the right track? How can you look for more of those signs?

Recognizing our successes also makes us more creative, confident, and courageous.

We start to see ourselves as resilient problem-solvers rather than screw-ups. We have compassion for mistakes, and help ourselves get right back on track. We're our own best friends.

Celebration is more fun and inspiring than being a downer.

And celebration is free. You can celebrate as much as you like. Who doesn't love more fun?

Everything counts

Today, practice relentlessly pointing out and recognizing your progress.

Notice and name every tiny shred of progress and success, no matter how small.

Be proud of yourself.

Relax. Play.

Reconnect with friends and family.

Enjoy.
