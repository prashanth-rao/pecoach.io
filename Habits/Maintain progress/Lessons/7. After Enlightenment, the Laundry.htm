Welcome to the world of maintenance.

People often imagine that "maintenance"—i.e., staying in a place where you want to be—is tedious and boring.

Or that it just suddenly happens, like a trap door opens and—plop!—you land in it.

Or that it's some kind of magical destination without problems.

That's probably not true.

It is true that when you're maintaining daily practices, you can think less about learning new things. You can cruise and relax more.

You can easily stay at a basic skill level (eating the right foods, at the right times, in the right amounts), and be quite happy and lean. You never need to "progress" more.

However, even as healthy daily practices come easier, you'll still need to support them.

Practices require reinforcement. Doing them every day is the most reliable way to do that.

Maintenance, then, is a commitment to working on daily healthy practices… for life.

  • To get lean, chop wood and carry water. (Or do your workouts and practices.)
  • To stay lean, chop wood and carry water. (Or do your workouts and practices.)

Maintenance is an ongoing journey. Every day is a new step. Even if the steps are comfortable and the road is clear... you still need to keep stepping forward.
