No Failure, Only Feedback

Replace “failure” with “information”.

Coach's Notes: Reviewing concept of "no failure, only feedback" -- how did actions lead to outcomes, and what does that tell us?

Explore and analyze your progress.

If you're ever feeling that you aren't totally happy with your progress, remember... all of this is a path to self-knowledge.

You can ask yourself some questions:

  • Do your expectations of progress match reality? How do you know?
  • Were you doing all the behaviors that truly mattered, consistently?
  • Could you be doing one or more of the basic practices a little bit better, or more often?
  • Are you looking in the wrong place for progress and happiness? Where else could you look?

If you are happy with something, that means you've found a strategy or mindset that did work for you.

Both things are self-knowledge. Both things help us learn to be better in future.

There is no failure, only feedback.

Accept the feedback, learn from it, and go forward with fresh perspective.

Ask yourself, "How's this working for me?"

  • If the answer is "Fabulously," then keep doing it.
  • If the answer is "Not so great," then use that information to change course. No big deal.

The sailboat

Ever seen a sailboat sail into the wind?

It rarely follows a straight course. Instead, it "tacks" from side to side, like a zigzag.

When you're sailing into the wind — i.e., having adventures and charting your new life course — you'll need to zig and zag a bit. You zig till you feel you've lost your wind, then you zag back. On average, you're going straight forward.

Feedback tells you when to zig or zag. That's all.

Thinking back over the last few months, what three things didn't go as well as you would have liked?

What feedback did these three things provide? In other words, what did these three things teach you about yourself? What did you learn from them?

Now that you've gotten this feedback, what could you change in future?
