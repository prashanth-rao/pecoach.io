Practice Letting Go

…and nobody will get hurt.

Coach's Notes: What is your "Dumbo's feather" -- the things you use as "magic talismans" to create control and order (but which don't actually do anything)?

Are you giving your power away to something outside yourself?

Remember the Disney movie Dumbo? When Dumbo the elephant receives a "magic feather" that allows him to fly?

Dumbo can already fly. The feather is just a plain old feather.

Dumbo's feather is a talisman that allows him to believe in himself. In a sense, he "outsourced" self-confidence to the feather.

Eventually, of course, Dumbo realizes he can fly all on his own.

This story reminds us of the importance of internal motivation—the power that comes from believing in what we're capable of. Not giving our power to something outside ourselves.

One way we can see what we're really capable of is to practice taking away our "Dumbo's feather".

What's your Dumbo's feather?

When it comes to fitness and nutrition, people have all kinds of magic feathers.

Such as:

  • a particular mealtime ritual
  • a particular combination of macronutrients
  • a particular range of foods
  • a particular time of day to exercise
  • a particular type of exercise

For example:

Some people will swear that running 5 miles a day is the only thing that keeps them lean.

Or that they have to do a triathlon—otherwise it means they aren't really "an athlete".

Some people shudder at the thought of a meal without meat, while other people shudder at the thought of a meal with meat.

Some people plan their menus with calculators and spreadsheets and food scales. Other people freak out when a number touches their food. (Generally, we'd say that having too many numbers lying around the kitchen is a public health hazard.)

Some people's magic feather is a scale weight, or a piece of clothing. If they stay that scale weight, or fit into that piece of clothing, it means they are officially "lean". (And if they don't... oy. The wailing.)

Notice what magic feathers have in common?

1.   They're all-or-nothing—either you have them or you don't.

2.   They're a collection of rules and "shoulds".

3.   They come from outside of you.

4.   They give something else that isn't you power over your behaviors and thoughts.

It's not heavy, it's my feather

You may, in fact, have settled into a good routine that works for you and your body. You may indeed have hit the sweet spot.

Alternatively, if you're feeling stagnant, it's also possible that you may also be clutching the feather a little too tightly. Relying on it a little too much.

Feel how much tension that inspires, when you squeeze that feather in a white-knuckle grip.

How do your routines make you feel?

Does grasping to routines make you anxious, exhausted, or afraid?

Do you want to let the feather go?

If you're feeling like the feather's no longer serving you, consider letting it go.

Have you gotten to a place that you're strong enough to go without it?

Maybe test it.

Or, if you love that feather, hang on to it. But relax your fingers. Hold the feather gently.
