Finding Internal Drive

Shifting from external to internal motivation.

Go inside

At some point in everyone's journey, the high fives die down.

Let them.

Yes, we want to encourage each other. To celebrate our successes. Brag a bit about our new accomplishments.

Then, at some point we have to turn our backs to the cheering crowd, and find acknowledgement from within.

From internal cheerleaders.

Tune in to your own instinct, and your own internal reward systems.

Find your "inner carrot" of meaningful motivation rather than chasing someone else's approval.

It may be uncomfortable. The outcome of this practice is a confidence that is authentic to who you are—not a confidence that depends on what others are saying.

That's freedom.

First: Some honesty.

Revise and resubmit

If you've ever published a paper in an academic or scientific journal, you'll know these three words: "revise and resubmit".

It means edit your work substantially, fix a bunch of stuff, then hand it back in—and maybe this time the editors and reviewers won't scribble all over it with red corrective marker.

Many writers dread these three words. They think it means their genius has gone unnoticed. Usually, all it means is that the paper needs a bit more work. Sometimes just a little bit of rewriting to take out the speling erurs. That's it.

Once the grumpy author gets over their snit fit, they might realize that the reviewers were right.

Today, double check with yourself to see whether your mission statement needs to be revised and resubmitted.

Use your mind-body scan

A mind-body scan can help. Use it to tune in to your internal cues.

How has your coaching journey been working for you? Do your goals, values, and priorities still resonate strongly with you?

Check with your body.

Does your mission statement feel right? Does it feel active and energizing? Or depleting and empty?

Check with your emotions.

Does it make you feel excited? Anxious? Obligated? Happy? Existentially itchy?

Check with your thoughts.

Are you over-thinking your mission statement? Fretting about details? Or is it all falling into place?

What do your body, emotions, and thoughts tell you about how well this mission statement works for you, your body, and your values?

Does it fit?

Revise, resubmit and respond

If you need to, streamline and simplify that mission statement. Bring it alongside so that you are sailing in the same direction.

It's OK if you need to negotiate your mission statement a little bit, rewrite it completely if you want. You haven't ordered it printed on business cards yet. It's still a work in progress.

Most importantly, tune in and follow your internal compass. This mission statement is about you, your body, and what's important to you.
