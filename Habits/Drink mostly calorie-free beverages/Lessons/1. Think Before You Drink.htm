Think Before You Drink

Make each drink a conscious choice, rather than a mindless one.

Coach's Notes: Explains rationale for habit; asks clients to simply consider whether their drink choices are helping them move towards their goals.

Are the calories that you're drinking making you better?

To bring more attention to the quality of what you're drinking, start with a couple of key questions.

Do the calories you're drinking …

  • Add value to your body and life? Or detract value?
  • Make your body, performance, and/or recovery better? Or worse?

To experiment, slow down. Pause before you drink to consider those questions. Aim to make the next beverage you drink a conscious, deliberate choice towards your goals, rather than automatic.

Add water

To move along the continuum of better, try substituting water for your normal calorie-containing beverages (such as juice or Gatorade) to stay hydrated.

Make it easy for yourself:

·         Start your morning with a glass of water.

Leave an empty glass on your bathroom sink so you'll see it when you wake up in the morning. Before you jump in the shower, fill the glass and drink.

·         Sip water between bites of food.

This will also help you slow your eating, and eat more consciously.

·         Buy a slick water bottle and carry it with you.

Or recycle a glass bottle or jar.

Make choices on a continuum

The practice of exploring no-calorie beverages is a game of pushing boundaries and building awareness.

It's not a set of rules, or restrictions on what's good or bad.

It's a game for you to figure out how to move along your continuum, according to what "a little bit better" is for you.

Keep it small and simple

Play with this experiment one small step at a time, and slowly level-up your no-calorie-beverage skills.

For example:

  • If you normally have a double-cream coffee, try cutting to one cream or using homogenized milk instead.
  • If you normally have a full-fat latte, try a skim milk latte or just a regular coffee.
  • If you normally have regular soda, try a diet soda. If you normally drink diet soda, try soda water with some lemon or lime.
  • If you normally have 3 glasses of wine in the evening, cut it to 1 or 2 glasses.
  • If you're looking for something a little tougher, try cold turkey on caloric drinks.

It's up to you.

Whatever you choose…

Experiment with being a calorie-free drinker.

Try it. Explore substitutions and small changes. Challenge yourself, if you want. Observe what happens.

Observe where you get annoyed, irritated, or resistant. (Like, "Nobody's gonna take away my beer!")

Likewise, see where you can stretch or nudge into small changes. If you practice consistently, over time those small changes accumulate to major progress.
