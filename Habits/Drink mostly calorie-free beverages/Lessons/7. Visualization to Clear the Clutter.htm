Visualization to Clear the Clutter

Clear your mental clutter and throw away the things that are holding you back.

Clear the clutter.

One way you can use visualization is to do a "mental makeover".

Think of this sort of like the brain equivalent of one of those home-makeover reality TV shows.

If your head was a room and you walked inside, how does it look? Clean and organized? Or like a house on Hoarders?

If you're feeling the clutter, try organizing one corner.

Visualization can help.

Shut your eyes and visualize your "mental attic".

Of all the clutter that was loved one day: the ancient yearbooks, stacks of moldy board games and creepy one-eyed dolls.

Start by creating a vivid, rich picture of your imaginary "mental attic".

  • What's in there?
  • What does it look like? smell like?
  • Can you feel the gritty dust under your feet? What’s that?

Spend a bit of time building this image until you can experience it clearly.

Look around.

You're looking for something in particular.

It's a box labeled Things That Are Holding Me Back.

Maybe there are several boxes. (Luckily you labeled them when you stored them up here.)

These boxes are full of things that are preventing you from moving forward with your journey in deep health and body transformation.

Like, maybe ...

  • an old photo, of someone in fifth grade calling you "fat".
  • a memory of falling down in gym class, or being picked last for a game.
  • there's a photo of you in great shape, something you never thought you could achieve again.
  • one of your negative "inner critics" — the voice in your head that reminds you about all the ways you suck.
  • an outfit you wore when you were younger, and you're convinced you'll never fit into it again.
  • an old diary or notebook in which you wrote all your excuses for not doing things.

Whatever it is, dig in the imaginary box. Pull out one thing that you know is holding you back.

Imagine yourself walking to a trash can, and throwing that thing out.

It's gone.

Pause to enjoy the relief and freedom.

You're free of one more little piece of mental junk that held you back.

Notice how it feels to let that thing go.
