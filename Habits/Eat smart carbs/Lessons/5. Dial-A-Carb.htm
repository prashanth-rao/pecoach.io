Dial-A-Carb

Expand your smart carb menu by dialling your carbs up or down.

Coach's Notes: "Dialing" carbs up or down in a meal; simply playing with macronutrient composition (without calling it that)

Carbohydrates aren’t an all-or-nothing game.

If you’re new to improving your carbohydrate intake:

Focus on:

  • Making each carb choice just a little bit better.
  • Experimenting with options that work for you.
  • Getting an appropriate portion size for your needs.

For most women, about 1 cupped handful of slow-digesting, nutrient-rich, high-fiber smart carbs per meal is a great starting point.

For most men, about 2 cupped handfuls of smart carbs per meal may be closer to appropriate servings.

Depending on your size, goals, and preferences, start with about 1-2 servings per meal, pay attention to how that affects you, and adjust with your coach accordingly.

Think on a continuum.

Not every meal will have the “perfect” amount of smart carbs. (Partly because there’s no such thing as "perfect"...)

You might not even want smart carbs at some meals. Maybe you just feel like having a salad and some shrimp or tofu right now.

That’s where guidelines and a template wins: it isn't rules. It's a loose blueprint that you can play with and adjust. Experiment to find what you like. Keep gathering data about what works for you.

Dial the carbohydrates up or down.

As you move along the continuum, you can try adjusting the amount of carbohydrate, the type, and even when you eat them (for instance, more with some meals rather than others).

If you dial up (i.e. more carbs), add higher-fiber, slow-digesting "smart carbs" such as starchy tubers, whole grains, beans and legumes, and/or fruit.

If you dial down (i.e. fewer carbs), stuff those meals full of delicious colorful veggies.

Learning adaptability in meals and menus.

Neither high nor lower carb menus are "good" or "bad” for everyone all the time. (However, most people don’t do well with no or very low carbs in their diets.)

By experimenting with options you're expanding your awareness and exploring what works for you.

Experiment. And have fun.
