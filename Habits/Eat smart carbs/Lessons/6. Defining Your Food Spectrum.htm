Defining Your Food Spectrum

Instead of focusing on "good" or "bad" foods, try to make your choices just a little bit better.

Practice thinking of all food on a spectrum from better to worse.

Rather than foods being black or white, “good” or “bad” ...

  • Some foods feel good and work well for you. They add health, boost your performance, improve your recovery, and/or help your body do its job. (Better.)
  • Some foods don’t feel good and don’t work well for you. They take away health, impair your performance, and/or clog up your body’s machinery. (Worse.)

It’s not “all-or-nothing”. There’s no “best” or “perfect” food or meal. There’s no rules or “shoulds”.

Instead:

We make choices in each situation, trying to move along the continuum to “just a little bit better”, as often as possible.

What’s your criteria for what makes a food “better” or “worse”?

For instance:

  • Gives me energy (better) versus takes energy away (worse).
  • Lots of health-promoting nutrients (better) versus few to no nutrients (worse).
  • Digests well (better) versus upsets my stomach (worse).
  • I can eat it calmly and sanely (better) versus one bite leads to a binge (worse).

It’s your game. You can play it the way you want.

What’s “better” in a specific context? Why?

Each situation will have its own “game rules”.

A choice you make in an airport might be different than a choice in a restaurant, or at home.

See if you can put each choice on the food spectrum, and figure out why you'd put it there.

The point of the game isn't necessarily to always have "better" choices. (Although that's always a good goal.)

The more important point is to understand why. With the clarity of what “better” means to you, you can make your choices conscious and purposeful, rather than random, accidental, or "I dunno".

A few more questions to ask yourself as you decide what’s “better”:

What’s in the food?

  • How is this food made?
  • How processed is this food (or not)?
  • What's in this food? (Or not in it?)

How does this food work with me?

  • How does this food affect my body? How do I feel physically after eating it?
  • Does this food, fundamentally, nourish me? Does it add or subtract value?
  • Do I find it easy or difficult to eat this food slowly and sanely?
  • Do I truly enjoy or value this food?

Where does this food fit on a continuum of “worse” or “better”?

  • What’s the situation I’m in right now? What are the options I have available in that situation?
  • What are the “worse” or “better” options in this situation?
  • With some creativity, could I expand or improve those options?
